Car of the Week

McLaren P1

The McLaren P1 is a groundbreaking hybrid supercar that redefines the limits of performance and technology. Combining a twin-turbocharged 3.8-liter V8 engine with an electric motor, it produces a staggering 903 horsepower, allowing it to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 2.8 seconds. With its aerodynamic design, active aerodynamics, and cutting-edge engineering, the P1 offers a thrilling driving experience while showcasing McLaren's commitment to innovation and efficiency. This automotive masterpiece not only dominates the track but also stands as a symbol of luxury and exclusivity.

Our Collection

Lexus LFA

The Lexus LFA is a remarkable supercar that combines luxury and performance in a stunning package. With its powerful 4.8-liter V10 engine, it delivers exhilarating speed and precision handling.

Lamborghini Aventador

The Lamborghini Aventador is an iconic supercar that embodies the essence of Italian engineering and design. Powered by a formidable 6.5-liter V12 engine, it roars with 730 horsepower, propelling you from 0 to 60 mph in just 2.9 seconds.

Ferrari 360

The Ferrari 360 is a classic sports car that represents the perfect blend of elegance and performance. Launched in 1999, it features a lightweight aluminum body and a 3.6-liter V8 engine, producing 400 horsepower and allowing it to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 4.2 seconds.

Toyota Supra Mark IV

The Toyota Supra Mark IV is a legendary sports car that has gained cult status among automotive enthusiasts. Produced from 1993 to 2002, it features a powerful 3.0-liter inline-six engine, offering an impressive 276 horsepower and remarkable tuning potential.

Nissan GT-R R34

The Nissan GT-R R34 is an iconic sports car that has become a symbol of performance and engineering excellence. Produced between 1999 and 2002, it features a robust 2.6-liter inline-six twin-turbocharged engine, delivering 280 horsepower and exceptional acceleration.

Porsche 911 Carrera GTS

The Porsche 911 997 Carrera GTS is a stunning blend of performance and heritage, representing the pinnacle of the 997 generation. Introduced in 2010, it features a 3.8-liter flat-six engine that produces 408 horsepower, allowing it to sprint from 0 to 60 mph in just 4.2 seconds.
